Define THE HEMATOCRIT (H) and state its formular
✓ Hematocrit (packed cell volume) of a sample is the ratio of the volume of erythrocytes to that of the whole blood.
✓ expressed as a percentage or preferably, as a decimal fraction
Differentiate Venous Hematocrit from Body Hematocrit
✓ Venous Hematocrit measures the proportion of red blood cell to plasma in the peripheral blood, but not in the entrie circulation.
✓ Body Hematocrit is the ratio of the total erythrocyte mass to the total blood volume
Describe how the procedures taken to determine the Hematocrit (H)
✓A blood sample is placed in a tube called Wintrobe tube that contains an anticoagulant and is then centrifuged for 30 mins at 3000 to 3500 rpm
✓ The erythrocytes will be packed at the bottom of the tube and above them is a layer of leukocytes, platelets and nucleated erythrocytes called the buffy layer (coat).
✓ Above the buffy coat is relatively cell-free plasma
From observing the hematocrit how would you be able to know the following; (1) Normal blood of a Male and that of a female. (2) A Person with Anemia (3) A Person with Polycythemia
✓ a normal male will have 42% - 52% Hematocrit
✓ a normal female will have 37% - 47% Hematocrit
✓ Anemia » a Depressed Hematocrit %
✓ Polycythemia » An elevated Hematocrit %
What is the clinical importance of Hematocrit determination
✓ Can be used to measure Renal Blood Flow
✓ is a good simple test for anemia or Polycythemia
✓ can be used to estimate the blood volume
✓ Gives a rough estimate of the size of erythrocytes and the concentration of erythrocytes.
